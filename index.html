  • For Free

    Denver’s Wedding Wednesdays program to offer free marriage ceremonies

    Denver Post file Rings at Denver Clerk & Recorder’s Office

    Denver couples looking to tie the knot without tying up their bank account can soon take advantage of the city’s free Wedding Wednesdays ceremonies.

    Denver’s Office of the Clerk and Recorder on Oct. 8 will launch the state’s first pilot program allowing couples a free wedding ceremony in its conference room, said office spokesman William Porter.

    Couples can make reservations for the 20-minute ceremonies, which will take place between 11 a.m. and 1 p.m. The ceremonies will be performed by a volunteer judge, magistrate or minister, Porter said. Twenty guests can attend the ceremony, and Porter said the office will try to provide for requests by the couple. Spanish-speaking ceremonies are also available.

    Denver Post file

    It was a record year for love in Denver.

  • Same-Sex

    Planning a same-sex wedding: Different challenges, still love

    Brian Kraft Photography Friends with parasols stand by as Sarah Scott and Erin Mills celebrate their wedding.

    Sarah Scott and Erin Mills still remember the first time they went to look at wedding dresses. They were in Loveland for the weekend and stopped into a national bridal store to check out what kind of things were available. It turned awkward, quickly.

    “At first, they thought we were sisters shopping together, and then they thought, ‘Oh, you’re getting married on the same day,’” Scott said. “Then it hit them, and everyone’s face turned red.”

    “We said, ‘No, we’re getting married to each other,’ ” Mills said. "The girl ran into the back to tell everyone what was going on."

    Scott and Mills were legally married in New York in April 2012, but the anniversary they celebrate every year followed a few months later.
